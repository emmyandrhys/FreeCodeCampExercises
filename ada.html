Ada Lovelace

The Gist of Ada

Ada Lovelace is often times considered the first computer programmer and software developer because of her writing about Charles Babbage's Difference Engine. She also was the mother of three children and woman struggling to avoid her father's "insanity" while being insane about mathematically determining how the world and the mind worked.

Important events in Ada's life

Dec. 10, 1815

Augusta Ada Byron is born to Lord Byron and Lady Byron

April 21, 1816

Lord Byron signs a Deed of Separation, essentially divorcing Lady Byron

April 19, 1824

Lord Bryon dies from a fever contracted while fighting in the Greek War for Independence

June 1829

Ada is paralyzed from a bout with measles

1831

Ada is recovered enough to be able to walk with the aid of crutches

February 1832

Ada designs and contructs a pair of wings

June 1833

Ada is introduced to Charles Babbage by her tutor Mary Sommerville and he invited her to see is prototype of the difference engine

July 8, 1835

Augusta Ada Byron marries Hon. William King

May 12, 1836

Ada gives birth to Byron King

September 22, 1837

Ada gives birth to Anne Isabella (called Annabella)

1838

William King becomes the Earl of Lovelace, making Ada the Countess of Lovelace

July 2, 1839

Ada gives birth to Ralph Gordon

1842-1843

Ada spends 9 months translating Luigi Menabrea's article on Babbage's machine, and her copious notes include the first "computer program"

1851

Ada develops a mathematical model for gambling but it goes wrong and she ends up owing thousands of pounds.

November 27, 1852

Ada dies at the age of 36 from uterine cancer
